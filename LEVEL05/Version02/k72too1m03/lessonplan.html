Module Aim & Objectives

Overall aim of the module, followed by the general objective and specific objectives for Mathematics for Computing.

Module Aim

To provide students with the core mathematical foundations required to understand data representation, numeric computation and basic statistics used in computing.

General Objective

At the end of this module, students will be able to apply number systems, set theory, statistical measures and computer coding concepts to analyse and solve basic computing‑related problems.

Specific Objectives

  • Convert numbers between decimal, binary, octal and hexadecimal (including fractional values) and perform base‑specific arithmetic.
  • Use complements (one’s and two’s complement) for signed number representation and binary subtraction.
  • Explain and apply fixed‑point and floating‑point number representations in a computing context.
  • Apply basic set theory concepts and notations relevant to algorithms and data structures.
  • Calculate and interpret basic statistical measures (mean, median, mode, standard deviation) for data sets used in computing.
  • Identify and describe common computer codes (BCD, EBCDIC, ASCII, Unicode) and their usage in data representation.
Lesson Plans (Week-wise)

Detailed lesson plans for each 1.5‑hour session, aligned with the training plan.

Week 1 – Number systems: introduction

General Objective

To introduce number systems used in computing and distinguish positional from non‑positional systems.

Specific Objectives

  • Define a number system and give everyday examples.
  • Differentiate positional and non‑positional number systems.
  • Identify factors affecting the value of a digit (base, position, digit).

Lesson Plan

TIME TOPIC TEACHER ACTIVITY STUDENT ACTIVITY TEACHING AIDS
20 min Motivation & everyday counting Relate counting in daily life (money, time, calendars) to number systems; elicit prior knowledge. Listen, share examples, respond to questions. PPT, whiteboard
40 min Positional vs non‑positional Explain place value, base and symbols; compare decimal with Roman numerals. Identify given systems as positional/non‑positional; take notes. Slides, board examples
30 min Practice & recap Give short questions to classify systems and explain value of digits; summarise key points. Answer questions individually; participate in recap discussion. Worksheet, board

Assessment

Students will be assessed through oral questioning and a brief written exercise on types of number systems and positional value.

Week 2 – Decimal to binary conversion

General Objective

To enable students to convert decimal integers to binary accurately.

Specific Objectives

  • Explain the repeated division by 2 method.
  • Convert positive decimal integers to binary.
  • Verify conversions by reconverting binary to decimal.

Lesson Plan

TIME TOPIC TEACHER ACTIVITY STUDENT ACTIVITY TEACHING AIDS
20 min Method explanation Introduce repeated division by 2; show simple example. Observe steps; ask clarification questions. Board, marker
40 min Guided conversions Solve several examples on board with class participation. Work along in notebooks; volunteer answers. PPT / board
30 min Individual practice Distribute worksheet with mixed decimal values; monitor and support. Complete worksheet; check answers with teacher. Worksheet

Assessment

Students will be assessed through the marked worksheet on decimal‑to‑binary conversions.

Week 3 – Decimal to octal and hexadecimal

General Objective

To develop skills in converting decimal numbers to octal and hexadecimal.

Specific Objectives

  • Use repeated division to convert decimal to octal and hexadecimal.
  • Relate octal and hexadecimal numbers to binary groupings.
  • Check answers using the reverse conversion.

Lesson Plan

TIME TOPIC TEACHER ACTIVITY STUDENT ACTIVITY TEACHING AIDS
25 min Decimal to octal Explain and demonstrate conversion using division by 8. Follow examples; note steps. Board, slides
35 min Decimal to hexadecimal Demonstrate conversion using division by 16; emphasise hex digits A–F. Answer guided questions; practise in pairs. PPT, board
30 min Binary grouping link Show how binary groups map to octal/hex; give short practice set. Complete practice; present answers. Worksheet

Assessment

Students will complete a homework / tutorial sheet on decimal–octal–hex conversions for checking next week.
