User Requirements for Software Solution

Module Code: K72T001M01 / Level 05  |  Module Type: Compulsory  |  Relevant Unit/s: K72T001U01

General Objective

At the end of this module, students will be able to understand and describe business processes of selected organizations, gather and analyse user requirements, and evaluate the feasibility of proposed software solutions.

Specific Objectives

At the end of this module the student shall be able to:

  1. Explain the business processes of a selected domain.
  2. Prepare and present a requirement analysis report of the selected project.
  3. Prepare and present a feasibility study report of the selected project.

Lesson Plans

Week 1 – Introduction to Business Process

General Objective
  • To introduce the concept of business processes and their importance in organizations.
  • To develop foundational understanding of structured business activities.
Specific Objectives
  • Define business process.
  • Explain purpose of business operations.
  • Identify real-life business process examples.
  • Describe the Input–Process–Output (IPO) model.
Time (min) Topic Teacher Activity Student Activity Aids
0–60 Introduction to Business Explain concept of business with simple local examples; elicit prior knowledge. Participate in discussion; share examples; respond to questions. Whiteboard
60–120 Business Process Definition Provide structured explanation of business process; highlight key terms. Listen and take notes; clarify doubts. Slides
120–180 IPO Model Demonstrate IPO model using one or two processes. Identify input, process and output elements from examples. Smart board
180–240 Case Example Present a short case scenario. Analyze the example in pairs; map steps as a process. Case sheet
240–270 Summary Review main points; clarify doubts. Ask questions; summarise key learning. Board
Assessment

Oral questioning and a short written task on key definitions and IPO model.

  • To enable classification of different types of processes.
Specific Objectives

  • Create a simple business process diagram.
Time (min) Topic Teacher Activity Student Activity Aids

60–120 Process Types Provide examples of core, support and management processes. Participate in discussion; classify examples. Whiteboard

240–270 Review Provide feedback on diagrams; recap key points. Participate in quick review. Board
Assessment

Evaluation of individual process diagrams and brief explanation of process type.
